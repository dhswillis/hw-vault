# Home Manager — Services Catalog

The 13 service lines, grouped by practice area. Each entry captures: what's in scope, what's explicitly out, how it's delivered (W-2 vs. vendor broker), and open questions.

## Food & Wellness

### 1. Food
- **1a. Nutritionist** — monthly consultation, macro/calorie targets, dietary restrictions, supplement guidance. Likely partnership with a local RD rather than in-house.
- **1b. Chef** — weekly meal prep in client's kitchen, or delivered. Open: direct-hire private chef vs. partnership with a chef collective? Scope creep risk (dinner parties, travel).
- **1c. Meal plans** — the plumbing layer: takes nutritionist's targets, translates into weekly shopping lists, hands off to chef or to household-operations layer for grocery delivery.

### 12. Fitness
- **12a. Personal training** — in-home or coordinated at client's gym. Likely vendor partnership.
- **12b. Group schedule decisions** — manage membership(s), class bookings across kids/adults, avoid double-booking.
- **12c. Home fitness equipment** — sourcing (new purchases), setup, maintenance, repair/replace cycle.

## Home Operations

### 2. Car cleaning
- Recurring exterior/interior detail on client's vehicles (personal + spouse + teen drivers). Mobile detailer partnership. Out: full paint correction, ceramic coating (one-off project scope).

### 3. Dry cleaning
- Pickup/delivery from client home to a trusted local cleaner. Includes tailoring coordination.

### 4. Laundry
- In-home (household laundry run by staff) or send-out (wash/fold service). Open: which model by default? In-home is premium, send-out is volume.

### 5. Home deep clean
- Quarterly deep clean layered on top of regular weekly cleaning. Windows, baseboards, inside-appliance, cabinets. Typically a separate vendor from weekly cleaner.

### 6. Home chores
- The ongoing weekly/biweekly household labor — cleaning, trash, restocking pantry/paper goods/toiletries, making beds, handling returns/packages. This is the "housekeeper" line.

### 8. Yard service
- Mowing, edging, leaf cleanup, seasonal cleanups. Recurring vendor. Distinct from landscaping (design/install work).

### 10. Landscaping
- Design, install, plant replacement, irrigation, mulching. Project-based. Higher-end vendor than yard service.

### 9. Gas orders
- **TBD — needs clarification from Harrison.** Candidates: propane tank refill (grill, fire pit, backup generator), home heating fuel delivery, or fuel-up service for vehicles. Most likely propane given fireplace/grill in the repair backlog.

## Home Repairs & Capital Projects

### 7. Home repairs and planning
- Ongoing handyman work + general-contractor coordination for larger projects. Single line of accountability for all trades (plumber, electrician, HVAC, roofer, drywall, tile, glazier).
- Client's current backlog lives in [[personal/projects/home-manager/home-repair-backlog]] — that doc is the running example of what this line delivers.

## Family & Transport

### 11. Childcare
- **11a. Nanny** — full-cycle: sourcing, background check, onboarding, payroll (via partner service), performance management.
- **11b. Drop off options** — carpool coordination, rideshare-for-minors accounts, backup-care network for sick days / school closures.
- Out of scope (likely): tutoring, therapy, medical appointments — too specialized, refer out.

### 13. Car maintenance
- Oil changes, tire rotation, state inspection, scheduled service at dealer/indie shop. Includes dropping car off and picking up. Out: collision repair (insurance process, refer out).

## Cross-cutting capabilities

Not service lines themselves, but required to deliver all 13:

- **Vendor bench** — vetted, insured providers per line per geography
- **Scheduling** — single calendar that coordinates across services (e.g. don't schedule deep clean the same morning as plumber)
- **Billing** — consolidated monthly invoice; clients should not see 13 different vendor bills
- **Reporting** — monthly household report: what was done, what's upcoming, what's flagged
- **Intake / asset inventory** — when a new client signs, first 30 days is inventorying the property (equipment, systems, warranties, service history)

## Open questions for scope definition

- Does Home Manager touch **financial admin** (bill-pay, subscription audits, insurance review)? Natural adjacency, not in current list.
- Does Home Manager touch **travel** (booking, itineraries, house-sitting coordination while client away)? Common ask for this customer profile.
- Does Home Manager touch **pet care** (vet visits, boarding, grooming coordination)? Not listed but often bundled.
