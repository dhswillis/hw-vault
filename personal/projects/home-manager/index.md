# Home Manager — Business Overview

## One-line

A single-point-of-contact service that runs a household the way a chief of staff runs an executive's day — food, cleaning, repairs, kids, cars, fitness, yard. Client pays one provider; we coordinate vendors, schedules, and quality behind the scenes.

## Goal

Build a productized concierge / household-operations business around the "Home Manager" role. First customer is Harrison's own household — it doubles as proof-of-demand and the MVP. Scale from there.

## Why now

- Harrison's own [[personal/projects/home-manager/home-repair-backlog|home has 7+ open repair/upgrade items]] sitting across multiple trades with no one owning the coordination. That's the pain point the business solves.
- PE-backed operator background (Haven Park — multifamily/MHC asset management) means the mental model is already vendor-management-at-scale, just pointed at a single household instead of a portfolio.
- Local market in the Southeast has high-income professionals with no equivalent of Alfred/Hello Alfred; existing options are fragmented cleaners, handymen, and lifestyle-management firms priced for the ultra-wealthy.

## Positioning (working hypothesis)

> "You have a finance person, a lawyer, a doctor. You need a Home Manager."

Target customer: dual-income household, $300k–$1M HHI, two or more kids, one or more of (demanding career, frequent travel, large property). They can afford to outsource but currently burn weekends coordinating the seven trades it takes to keep a house running.

Not: cleaning-only, handyman-only, or ultra-luxury estate-manager (where client has a live-in staff).

## Business model — open questions

- **Revenue**: Flat monthly retainer (e.g. $1,500–$3,500/mo tiered) vs. retainer + pass-through vs. pure markup on vendor invoices?
- **Staffing**: W-2 employees (cleaners, handymen in-house) vs. broker model (curated vendor bench, we manage) vs. hybrid?
- **Geography**: Start where? One metro, one zip-code cluster, or Harrison's own neighborhood first?
- **Tech**: Client-facing app from day one, or CRM + Slack + Google Sheets until volume forces it?
- **Entity**: LLC vs. S-corp; does it sit under an existing Haven Park-adjacent entity or fully separate?
- **Brand**: Home Manager is the role, not necessarily the name. Naming TBD.

## MVP plan (draft — to pressure-test)

1. **Customer Zero**: Run the system against Harrison's own household for 60–90 days. Close the 7 open repair items, stand up recurring cleaning/laundry/yard, document every vendor, hour, and cost. See [[personal/projects/home-manager/home-repair-backlog]].
2. **Playbook**: Convert the Customer Zero ops into written SOPs — intake checklist, vendor scorecard, weekly-review template, monthly-report template.
3. **Customer One–Three**: Recruit 2–3 friendly customers at a friends-and-family rate. Refine pricing and scope.
4. **Decide**: Based on unit economics from the first 5 customers, decide whether this is a lifestyle business (stay small, high-margin) or a scalable platform (raise capital, hire ops lead, expand geography).
