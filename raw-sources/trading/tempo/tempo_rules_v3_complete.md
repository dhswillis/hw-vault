## 1. ENTRY RULES

### 1A. IFVG (Inverted Fair Value Gap) — Primary Entry Mechanism
- **Definition:** 3-candle pattern where middle candle creates a gap; price then closes through the gap to trigger entry
- **Optimal gap size:** 8–15 points on NQ
- **Acceptable gap size:** 5–6 points (risky but valid with additional confluence)
- **Strong setup gap size:** 20–30 points (high conviction)
- **DO NOT trade:** Gaps above 30 points (too much risk for stop placement)
- **Entry trigger:** Candle CLOSE above/below the gap (not just a wick touch)
- **Singular gaps preferred** — avoid entries when multiple FVGs are stacked (increases failure probability)
- **Reject FVG into IFVG** = strong confidence setup (higher timeframe rejection feeding into entry timeframe inversion)
- **Half-position entry:** Enter half on close, add second half on pullback/retest of gap
- **V3 UPDATE (Jul-Oct 2025):** 30-second IFVG became equally viable as 2-minute for entry — not just data wick situations
- **V3 UPDATE (Nov 2025+):** IFVG + SMT together = highest probability combination; IFVG alone without SMT dropped to ~60-65% WR

### 1B. Confirmation Patterns (Must See Before Entry)
- **V-shaped recovery with instant momentum** — strongest signature
- **Death candles through gaps** (80–100 point candles = high volatility confirmation)
- **Wick rejection without close past level** = major confidence signal
- **Three consecutive directional candles** after sweep = strong reaction
- **Instant directional candles after sweep** = bullish/bearish reversal signal
- **46+ point death candle** = exactly what you want to see for momentum confirmation
- **V3 UPDATE:** "Reaction is everything" — price reaction to sweep matters more than the sweep itself

### 1C. Setup Quality Classification (UPDATED V3 — Jan 2026)
- **A+ Setup (7+ confluences, 80%+ WR):** Major liquidity sweep (1+ hour old) + SMT divergence + singular IFVG 8-15pts + daily bias alignment + 50% level rejection with wick + clean momentum through gap + BPR support + session level alignment + multiple higher-TF confluences
- **A Setup (5-6 confluences, 70-75% WR):** Major sweep + IFVG + SMT + good momentum + bias alignment (may require chasing, order block nearby adds confidence)
- **B+ Setup (3-4 confluences, 50-70% WR):** Secondary liquidity sweep + smaller gaps (5–10 pts) + limited/no SMT
- **B Setup (2 confluences):** Missing major confluence factors — trade with reduced size only
- **C Setup / Skip (0-1 confluences):** DO NOT TRADE
- **V3 CRITICAL UPDATE:** By Jan 2026, Tempo raised minimum to trade from 2 confluences to 3+ for funded accounts

### 1D. Data Wick Setup (News-Based)
- **Trigger:** Triple red folder news (8:30 AM CPI, NFP, PPI, Unemployment Claims)
- **Formation:** One-minute candle with wicks on BOTH ends (top and bottom)
- **Ideal data wick size:** 50–100 points top-to-bottom
- **Strong data wick:** 70–80 points (good), 100+ points (mega gap)
- **Entry:** Wait for ONE side to fully sweep, then enter opposite direction
- **Target:** Opposite end of wick
- **Triple red folder = 70–80% chance** of data wick forming
- **V3 UPDATE (Apr-Jun 2025):** Data wick entries confirmed as HIGHEST WIN RATE setup type
- **V3 UPDATE:** 15-second timeframe is used ONLY for data wick entries — not for regular IFVG

### 1E. Data Candle Setup (V3 NEW — Nov 2025+)
- **Definition:** Trading the reaction candle from triple red folder news on 15-second timeframe
- **Entry:** Watch 15-second chart during 8:30 AM news; enter on the first clear directional close after initial spike
- **Formation probability:** 70-80% on triple red folder days
- **Win rate:** Consistently highest among all setup types
- **Risk:** Tight stops (10-15 pts) due to 15-second timeframe precision
- **Position size:** Normal size — high probability justifies full position
- **Key rule:** Only trade the FIRST data candle reaction; second attempts have lower probability

### 1F. Order Block Entry
- **Definition:** Down-closed candle followed by a green/golfing candle; draw a line at the body and enter when price retests
- **30-second order block:** Used for quick scalp entries after major moves
- **1-minute order block:** Standard confirmation entry
- **2-minute order block:** Higher timeframe entry with wider stops
- **Order block reentry:** After initial IFVG failure, down candle creates OB → reentry at OB works
- **Strong signal:** One-minute order block holding with multiple wicks = high confidence

### 1G. Chasing Rules (High Risk — UPDATED V3)
- **When to chase:** Major liquidity sweep + strong momentum + good reaction + clean gap + first rejection only
- **When NOT to chase:** Multiple FVGs stacked, close to open, small gaps, first 2 minutes of session
- **Chase technique:** Enter with 10–20 seconds left on candle, assuming favorable close
- **Size:** Half size FIRST when chasing, full size on retracement/confirmation
- **Exit rule:** Immediate exit if price reverses back into gap
- **Wait minimum:** 5+ minutes from open (let market settle)
- **V3 UPDATE (Apr 2025):** "Half size first, full on retracement" = standard chasing protocol

### 1H. BPR Entry (V3 NEW — Nov 2025+)
- **BPR (Balance Price Range):** Overlapping bullish and bearish FVGs creating a tight price zone
- **Tempo calls it:** "FVG on steroids"
- **Entry:** When price returns to BPR zone, enter in direction of bias
- **Stop:** Below/above BPR (typically tight — 10-15 pts)
- **Confluence:** BPR + SMT + liquidity sweep = A+ setup
- **Key:** BPR is a confirmation zone, not a standalone entry — needs additional confluences

## 3. STOP LOSS RULES (MAJOR V3 UPDATE)

- **V3 (Jul 2025+):** **Soft stops preferred ~90% of the time**

- **Soft stop method:** Watch price action at stop level; exit only if candle CLOSES past stop (not just wicks)
- **Hard stops used ONLY:** On funded accounts with strict drawdown rules, or when stepping away from screen
- **Apr 2025 discovery:** Hard stops reconsidered after series of getting wicked out

### 3B. Placement (In Order of Preference)
1. **Close above/below IFVG** = preferred mechanical stop (usually 20–30 points)

3. **Order block close** = usually 10–20 points

### 3C. Stop Sizing

- **Extend stop** if order blocks are close by ("extend your stop to include OB below/above")

### 3D. Break-Even Rules (CRITICAL — Non-Negotiable)

- **Result:** What looked like a loss becomes zero, preventing major drawdowns

### 3E. Mental/Soft Stops (V3 EXPANDED)

- **V3:** Soft stops are DEFAULT — hard stops are the exception

- Requires screen time — not suitable for walk-away trades

## 4. DAILY BIAS DETERMINATION

### 4A. Premium/Discount Framework
- **Calculation:** Draw from daily high to daily low, mark the 50% midpoint
- **Trading day starts at 6:00 PM EST previous day** (includes overnight/Asia)
- **Above 50%** = PREMIUM → short bias for reversion back to 50%
- **Below 50%** = DISCOUNT → long bias for reversion back to 50%
- **At 50%** = NO BIAS → wait for a sweep to establish direction
- **50% level** acts as reliable support/resistance; bounces here are common
- **Yellow line** marks 50% on chart

### 4B. First Sweep Rules
- First sweep direction determines ~70% of daily bias
- **Open in premium + first sweep of high** = bearish day likely
- **Open in discount + first sweep of low** = bullish day likely
- Shorting from premium back down to 50% = ideal setup
- Longing from discount back up to 50% = ideal setup

### 4C. Bias Disqualifiers
- **Do NOT long** heavily in deep premium without major sweep below
- **Do NOT short** heavily in deep discount without major sweep above
- **All overnight areas swept before market open** = worst conditions for the model (no edge)
- **"150 points in discount"** = heavy discount state, bullish bias strong

### 4D. Two-Level Waiting Strategy
- When two significant areas of liquidity exist above and below, wait for price to sweep one, then target the other
- Common pattern: sweep London low → target London high (or vice versa)

### 4E. Trend Day Protocol (V3 NEW — Jul-Oct 2025)
- **Definition:** Market opens and moves directionally all day with minimal pullback
- **Recognition:** Price breaks opening range aggressively, doesn't look back for 30+ minutes
- **Rule:** Take ONE trade in trend direction, then walk away
- **DO NOT:** Try to fade a trend day or catch the reversal
- **Frequency:** ~1-2 per month
- **Key indicator:** All-time highs with no sell-side liquidity below = potential trend day up

## 6. SMT (Smart Money Technique) RULES — V3 MAJOR UPGRADE

### 6A. Core Definition (UNCHANGED)
- **SMT = NQ vs ES divergence** at key liquidity levels
- When one index sweeps a liquidity level but the other doesn't = divergence signal
- Look at the SAME liquidity level on both ES and NQ charts
- Price doesn't need to hit exact tick — 1–2 ticks away still counts

### 6B. V3 STATUS: SMT IS NOW PRIMARY CONFLUENCE (Jan 2026)
- **V2 (Oct 2024 – Jun 2025):** SMT was one of several confluences, not required
- **V3 (Jul 2025+):** SMT importance gradually increased
- **V3 (Jan 2026):** SMT became the **PRIMARY confluence** — Tempo explicitly elevated it
- **Without SMT:** Win rate drops to ~60-65% (from 75-80% with SMT)
- **With SMT:** Win rate 80%+ across 400+ trades (Nov 2025 – Jan 2026)
- **"If you have SMT + IFVG + sweep, you're in the money most of the time"**

### 6C. Specific Signals
- **Bullish SMT:** NQ sweeps low but ES doesn't = longs valid (NQ is stronger upside)
- **Bearish SMT:** ES sweeps high but NQ doesn't = shorts valid (ES is weaker)
- **Open SMT:** Strongest signal — divergence visible from session open
- **Cross-index trading:** Can use ES liquidity sweep for London and take NQ short (done regularly)

### 6D. SMT Breaking
- Once BOTH indices sweep the same level = SMT is broken, need new setup
- Example: "SMT was valid until [level] got swept on both"

### 6E. Confluence (UPDATED)
- SMT + major sweep + IFVG = A+ setup (MINIMUM for highest confidence)
- SMT at entry point adds major confluence to any reversal bias
- Most effective within first 5–10 minutes of market open (higher volatility = more SMTs)
- **V3:** SMT alone without IFVG still has edge if paired with session level sweep
- **V3:** Absence of SMT is now treated as a NEGATIVE confluence (reduces position size)

### 10B. Power of Three (Market Structure)
1. **Accumulation:** Choppy, consolidating, tight ranges — no trades
2. **Manipulation:** Sharp move one direction, traps traders, sweeps liquidity — look for reversals
3. **Distribution:** Major move in intended direction to buy-side/sell-side liquidity — follow the move
- **Trade application:** Identify which phase market is in before entering
- **V3 NOTE:** Power of Three mentioned less frequently after Jun 2025; IFVG + SMT + sweep framework replaced it as primary model

### 10C. New Week Opening Gap (NWOG)
- Gap from Friday's close to Monday's open
- Price can bounce off NWOG and provide entry setups

### 10D. Trend Line Liquidity

- When price breaks trend and reverses, it seeks trend line liquidity
- Used as "major draw" in conjunction with IFVG entries
- 100+ points away from current price = higher conviction

### 10E. "Tempo Sweep" (Near-Miss)
- Price misses liquidity level by 1–2 ticks but still reverses as if it swept
- "Real thing that happens" — not a failure, just a near-sweep
- Treat as valid sweep for entry purposes

### 10F. Copy Trading Infrastructure (V3 NEW — Oct 2025+)
