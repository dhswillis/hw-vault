## 1. ENTRY RULES

### 1A. IFVG (Inverted Fair Value Gap) — Primary Entry Mechanism
- **Definition:** 3-candle pattern where middle candle creates a gap; price then closes through the gap to trigger entry
- **Optimal gap size:** 8–15 points on NQ
- **Acceptable gap size:** 5–6 points (risky but valid with additional confluence)
- **Strong setup gap size:** 20–30 points (high conviction)
- **DO NOT trade:** Gaps above 30 points (too much risk for stop placement)
- **Entry trigger:** Candle CLOSE above/below the gap (not just a wick touch)
- **Singular gaps preferred** — avoid entries when multiple FVGs are stacked (increases failure probability)
- **Reject FVG into IFVG** = strong confidence setup (higher timeframe rejection feeding into entry timeframe inversion)
- **Half-position entry:** Enter half on close, add second half on pullback/retest of gap

### 1B. Confirmation Patterns (Must See Before Entry)
- **V-shaped recovery with instant momentum** — strongest signature
- **Death candles through gaps** (80–100 point candles = high volatility confirmation)
- **Wick rejection without close past level** = major confidence signal
- **Three consecutive directional candles** after sweep = strong reaction
- **Instant directional candles after sweep** = bullish/bearish reversal signal
- **46+ point death candle** = exactly what you want to see for momentum confirmation

### 1C. Setup Quality Classification
- **A+ Setup:** Major liquidity sweep (1+ hour old) + SMT divergence + singular IFVG + daily bias alignment + 50% level rejection with wick + clean momentum through gap + multiple higher-TF confluences
- **A Setup:** Major sweep + IFVG + good momentum (may require chasing, order block nearby adds confidence)
- **B+ Setup:** Secondary liquidity sweep + smaller gaps (5–10 pts) + limited/no SMT (50–70% win rate historically, lower confidence)
- **B Setup:** Missing one major confluence factor — trade with reduced size only
- **C Setup / Skip:** Missing multiple factors — DO NOT TRADE

### 1D. Data Wick Setup (News-Based)
- **Trigger:** Triple red folder news (8:30 AM CPI, NFP, PPI, Unemployment Claims)
- **Formation:** One-minute candle with wicks on BOTH ends (top and bottom)
- **Ideal data wick size:** 50–100 points top-to-bottom
- **Strong data wick:** 70–80 points (good), 100+ points (mega gap)
- **Entry:** Wait for ONE side to fully sweep, then enter opposite direction
- **Target:** Opposite end of wick
- **Triple red folder = 70–80% chance** of data wick forming

### 1E. Order Block Entry
- **Definition:** Down-closed candle followed by a green/golfing candle; draw a line at the body and enter when price retests
- **30-second order block:** Used for quick scalp entries after major moves
- **1-minute order block:** Standard confirmation entry
- **2-minute order block:** Higher timeframe entry with wider stops
- **Order block reentry:** After initial IFVG failure, down candle creates OB → reentry at OB works
- **Strong signal:** One-minute order block holding with multiple wicks = high confidence

### 1F. Chasing Rules (High Risk)
- **When to chase:** Major liquidity sweep + strong momentum + good reaction + clean gap + first rejection only
- **When NOT to chase:** Multiple FVGs stacked, close to open, small gaps, first 2 minutes of session
- **Chase technique:** Enter with 10–20 seconds left on candle, assuming favorable close
- **Size:** Half size only when chasing
- **Exit rule:** Immediate exit if price reverses back into gap
- **Wait minimum:** 5+ minutes from open (let market settle)

## 2. EXIT / TRIM RULES

### 2A. Trim Strategy (Points Profit on NQ)
- **TP1:** 15–20 points → trim 20–50% of position (mechanical — non-negotiable)
- **TP2:** 30–40 points → trim additional 20–40%
- **TP3:** 50–70 points → trim 20–30%
- **Runner:** Hold remaining 1 micro minimum for 70–120+ point moves
- **Aggressive trim example:** Trim 70–80% at first target area, 20% at second, hold 10% for home run

### 2B. Named Targets (In Order of Frequency Used)
1. **50% of daily range** (yellow line — primary reversion target, very strong bounce area)
2. **Session highs/lows** (London H/L, Asia H/L, NY AM HOD)
3. **Data wick high/low** (from news events — 50–100 point targets)
4. **4H FVG levels** (major liquidity areas)
5. **Previous day high/low**
6. **Weekly highs/lows**
7. **Trend line liquidity** (100+ points away = higher conviction)
8. **Internal swing highs/lows** (relative equal highs/lows on 1m/5m)
9. **New week opening gap** (Monday gap from Friday close)

### 2C. Profit Target Philosophy
- **"1 win per day is enough"** — core discipline
- **Second trim rarely hits** — focus on first trim lock-in
- **When price goes 1–4 points past target then reverses** = manipulation, be aware
- **If within 1–2 points of TP:** Take profits immediately (no limit orders, just market out)
- **"Never let a green trade turn red"** — apply break-even aggressively after first TP

### 2D. Trail Stop Strategy
- After taking initial profits, trail stops on remaining position
- Trail in 10–15 point increments after first trim
- Hold runners with wider stops (swing-based, not fixed)
- One micro held for major liquidity target (London high, data wick, etc.)

## 3. STOP LOSS RULES

### 3A. Placement (In Order of Preference)
1. **Close above/below IFVG** = preferred mechanical stop (usually 20–35 points)
2. **Swing high/low stop** = usually 10–25 points
3. **Order block close** = usually 10–20 points
4. **Close above BPR** = tight confluence stop
5. **Tight stops (5–8 points)** = risky, get wicked easily — AVOID

### 3B. Stop Sizing
- **Typical stop:** 20–30 points
- **Small gap setups:** 8–12 point stop
- **Extended stops:** 35–45 points on high-conviction plays
- **Minimum comfortable:** 10+ points before considering break-even move
- **Extend stop** if order blocks are close by ("extend your stop to include OB below/above")

### 3C. Break-Even Rules (CRITICAL — Non-Negotiable)
- **Trigger 1:** 15–20 points profit secured → go break-even immediately
- **Trigger 2:** Hit internal swing high/low after entry → go break-even
- **Trigger 3:** Close above/below internal higher low → go break-even
- **Minimum for BE:** DO NOT go break-even at 5–10 points (too tight, will get wicked)
- **"Manipulation prevention":** Tight stops get wicked out; BE at 20pts saves accounts
- **Result:** What looked like a loss becomes zero, preventing major drawdowns
- **Real examples:** Saved from 30–40 point losses by going break-even at 20 points multiple times

### 3D. Mental Stops
- "Do not use hard stops on highly volatile days" — use mental stops instead
- Give more breathing room when volatility is extreme

## 4. DAILY BIAS DETERMINATION

### 4A. Premium/Discount Framework
- **Calculation:** Draw from daily high to daily low, mark the 50% midpoint
- **Trading day starts at 6:00 PM EST previous day** (includes overnight/Asia)
- **Above 50%** = PREMIUM → short bias for reversion back to 50%
- **Below 50%** = DISCOUNT → long bias for reversion back to 50%
- **At 50%** = NO BIAS → wait for a sweep to establish direction
- **50% level** acts as reliable support/resistance; bounces here are common
- **Yellow line** marks 50% on chart

### 4B. First Sweep Rules
- First sweep direction determines ~70% of daily bias
- **Open in premium + first sweep of high** = bearish day likely
- **Open in discount + first sweep of low** = bullish day likely
- Shorting from premium back down to 50% = ideal setup
- Longing from discount back up to 50% = ideal setup

### 4C. Bias Disqualifiers
- **Do NOT long** heavily in deep premium without major sweep below
- **Do NOT short** heavily in deep discount without major sweep above
- **All overnight areas swept before market open** = worst conditions for the model (no edge)
- **"150 points in discount"** = heavy discount state, bullish bias strong

### 4D. Two-Level Waiting Strategy
- When two significant areas of liquidity exist above and below, wait for price to sweep one, then target the other
- Common pattern: sweep London low → target London high (or vice versa)

## 6. SMT (Smart Money Technique) RULES

### 6A. Core Definition
- **SMT = NQ vs ES divergence** at key liquidity levels
- When one index sweeps a liquidity level but the other doesn't = divergence signal
- Look at the SAME liquidity level on both ES and NQ charts
- Price doesn't need to hit exact tick — 1–2 ticks away still counts

### 6B. Specific Signals
- **Bullish SMT:** NQ sweeps low but ES doesn't = longs valid (NQ is stronger upside)
- **Bearish SMT:** ES sweeps high but NQ doesn't = shorts valid (ES is weaker)
- **Open SMT:** Strongest signal — divergence visible from session open
- **Cross-index trading:** Can use ES liquidity sweep for London and take NQ short (done regularly)

### 6C. SMT Breaking
- Once BOTH indices sweep the same level = SMT is broken, need new setup
- Example: "SMT was valid until [level] got swept on both"

### 6D. Confluence
- SMT + major sweep + IFVG = A+ setup
- SMT at entry point adds major confluence to any reversal bias
- Most effective within first 5–10 minutes of market open (higher volatility = more SMTs)

## 7. RISK MANAGEMENT

### 7A. Position Sizing
- **$50K funded account:** 10–15 micro contracts typical
- **$150K funded account:** 15–20 micro contracts
- **Pre-market / risky setups:** 5–10 micros (half size)
- **Extreme volatility:** 4–8 micros (size down heavily)
- **Risk per trade:** 0.4–0.6% of account ($200–300 on $50K)
- **NOT 1%** — confirmed 0.4–0.6% is the actual risk

### 7B. Loss Management
- **Stop after 2 consecutive losses** for the day — non-negotiable
- **Max loss per trade:** 10–35 points typical
- **After loss:** Wait for A-setup only before next trade (not just any setup)
- **No revenge trading** — no scaling up after drawdown
- **"If you're going to blow accounts, step away"**

### 7C. Profit Protection
- **Risk only profits** on 2nd/3rd trade of day ("gambling profits, not account capital")
- **When up $150–300+:** Lock funded accounts, no more funded trades
- **"Only risking what I made on the first play"** = standard practice
- **After taking daily profit target:** "Close up funded accounts and secure a green day"

### 7D. Size Adjustments
- **Half size:** Bad conditions, choppy markets, thin volume, holiday periods
- **Full size:** Normal volatility, clear bias, A/A+ setups
- **Double caution:** Election weeks, FOMC days, December
- **Copy-trading multiplier:** 1 win × 5–10 accounts = profit scaling across prop firms

- Multiple gaps stacked above and below entry
- Too close to news event (within 2 minutes)
- Already 2+ losses in day → done trading
- Setup doesn't have SMT or major sweep confirmation
- Chop / range conditions (70–100 point range in 2+ hours = bad)
- Already taken profit target for day ("already green")
- All overnight areas swept before market open = worst conditions
- No clear bias / 50% level not respected
- "ES too close to [liquidity level]" = play invalidated
- First 2–5 minutes after open (too volatile, let market settle)
- Stacked gaps above AND below entry = high failure
- At all-time highs in premium with no sell-side liquidity

## 9. DRAW ON LIQUIDITY (DOL)

### 9A. Definition
- Where price is heading — the target that price wants to reach
- Merge IFVG entry with strong "draw" for high-probability trade

### 9B. Hierarchy of Draw Strength
1. **London high/low** = major draw (price will seek it)
2. **Data wick high/low** = very strong draw (50–100 pt targets)
3. **Hourly high/low** = strong draw
4. **50% of daily range** = moderate draw (tested frequently)
5. **Previous day high/low** = moderate draw
6. **Weekly highs/lows** = longer-term draw
7. **Trend line liquidity** = structural draw (100+ points away = higher conviction)
8. **4H FVG levels** = institutional draw

### 9C. Multiple Draw = Higher Probability
- When multiple draw-on-liquidity levels align = strongest setups
- "Combine the fact that we're tapping into this gap AND tapping off the 50% level = play justified"

## 10. ADDITIONAL CONCEPTS

### 10A. BPR (Breaker Point Reversal)
- **Definition:** Overlap of previous bullish and bearish timeframe gaps, creating tight entry/exit zone
- **Rule:** Avoid going long/short into tight BPRs (high failure rate)
- **"Do not short into a 5-minute BPR when it's this close"** — wait for BPR to be penetrated first
- BPR used as entry point when paired with order block retest

### 10B. Power of Three (Market Structure)
1. **Accumulation:** Choppy, consolidating, tight ranges — no trades
2. **Manipulation:** Sharp move one direction, traps traders, sweeps liquidity — look for reversals
3. **Distribution:** Major move in intended direction to buy-side/sell-side liquidity — follow the move
- **Trade application:** Identify which phase market is in before entering

### 10C. New Week Opening Gap (NWOG)
- Gap from Friday's close to Monday's open
- Price can bounce off NWOG and provide entry setups
- "New week opening gap that we can bounce off of and take setups on"
- Works as support/resistance in first hours of Monday trading

### 10D. Trend Line Liquidity
- Liquidity levels created along trend lines on hourly/4H charts
- When price breaks trend and reverses, it seeks trend line liquidity
- Used as "major draw" in conjunction with IFVG entries
- 100+ points away from current price = higher conviction

### 10E. "Tempo Sweep" (Near-Miss)
- Price misses liquidity level by 1–2 ticks but still reverses as if it swept
- "Real thing that happens" — not a failure, just a near-sweep
- Treat as valid sweep for entry purposes

## 12. TRADE MANAGEMENT

### 12A. During Trade
- Trim 30–50% at first level (15–20 points)
- Trail stop in 10–15 point increments after first trim
- Break-even after 20 points profit = non-negotiable
- Hold runners with wider stops (swing-based)

### 12B. Second Trade Protocol
- Only risk profits from first trade ("gambling profits")
- Half size on second trade of the day
- If second trade loses → DONE for the day
- "If I definitely took a loss on that second trade, I would have shut it down"

### 12C. Account Locking
- After reaching daily profit target → lock all funded accounts
- "I'm not going to now start spiraling out of control and give back all my weekly profits just to save the win streak"
- After 2 losses → "locked out all my accounts"
