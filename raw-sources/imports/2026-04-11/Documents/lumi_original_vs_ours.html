FVG+10 is our invention. The original Lumi spec says swing H/L for the stop. We tested FVG+10 as an alternative and it massively outperformed (62% WR, 2.76 PF vs 44% WR, 1.03 PF). This is the biggest divergence from the original rules and where the entire edge comes from.

Partials Not mentioned — appears to be all-or-nothing to 2R All-or-nothing: stop, target, or EOD flatten. No partials. MATCH
EOD flatten Not explicitly stated 20:55 UTC (3:55 PM EST) — close any open trade N/A
Ambiguity in Lumi's "2R from FVG". The original says "2R from FVG entry price" but doesn't define R. It could mean: (a) R = FVG gap size, so target = 2 × gap distance from FVG edge, or (b) R = FVG entry to stop, so target = 2 × risk from FVG. We implemented (b). With FVG+10 stop, R is small (~10pts), so target is ~20pts from FVG. With swing stop, R is larger (~15-25pts), so target is ~30-50pts.

Bottom line: The core flow (HTF level → M30/H1 sweep → M3/M5 OB → M3/M5 FVG → entry at FVG edge → 2R target) is faithfully implemented. The two biggest divergences are (1) the FVG+10pt stop instead of swing H/L — which is where all the edge comes from — and (2) how "R" is defined in the target calculation. Everything else is either a parameter choice that Lumi didn't specify, or a mechanical addition (fill checks, sanity guards) needed for automated backtesting.
