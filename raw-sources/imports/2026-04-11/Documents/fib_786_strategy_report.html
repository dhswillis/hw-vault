Strategy Overview

The Setup

1. Identify an impulse leg on the 5-minute chart — loose detection allows counter-candles within the leg; leg ends when price retraces >50% of wick range from the extreme

2. Draw fibonacci levels on the candle body (open→close, not wick→wick)

3. After the confirmation candle closes, check if price is at or below 0.786 of body

4. Place a limit order at 0.786 — stop at body 1.0 — risk = 0.214 × body_size

5. Target deeper retracement: 0.618, 0.500, 0.382, 0.236, or 0.000

Confirmation Candle Mechanics — Critical Finding

Where does the confirmation candle close relative to 0.786?

The confirmation candle (the 5M candle that triggers the leg-end by retracing >50% of wick) has two distinct behaviors:

78% of clean legs: The conf candle dips to 0.786 mid-candle (in its wick) but closes back ABOVE 0.786 near the body top. The limit order at 0.786 almost never fills in this group — only 5.9% fill rate. When it does fill (much later), there is zero edge (Z = -0.8, pure random walk).

22% of clean legs: The conf candle closes BELOW 0.786 of body. The limit fills essentially immediately (99.6% fill rate). ALL of the edge comes from this group — 96.4% WR on first target, Z = +21.1.

Implication: The limit order isn't providing special adverse-selection filtering. It's a proxy for detecting deeply retracing confirmation candles. The real signal is: "the confirmation candle closed past 0.786 of body."

Look-Ahead Bias Audit

Timeline verified clean:

1. 5M leg candles form (all historical data known at this point)

2. Confirmation candle closes — we wait for the full 5-minute close before acting

3. Calculate body fibs + place limit at 0.786 of body

4. Watch ticks AFTER confirmation close for fill (trade-through of 1 tick required)

5. Simulate from fill tick forward — stop at body 1.0, targets below

6. Intra-leg retrace filter: if 0.786 was already hit within leg formation, trade excluded

No information from the future is used at any decision point.

Concerns & Caveats

1. Small risk per trade: Avg 6.9 pts ($138/contract). Spread, slippage, and execution costs are proportionally larger at this scale. A 1-tick slip can eat 5-15% of risk.

2. 1-candle 5M legs dominate: 90% of fills come from single 5M candle legs. These are 5-minute impulse moves, not extended multi-bar trends.

3. Fill mechanics: The limit at 0.786 relies on price bouncing back to the level after the confirmation candle already passed through it. In fast markets, the bounce may not materialize or may gap past your level.

4. Confirmation candle self-selection: Only 22% of clean legs actually produce fills. The 78% that don't fill (conf candle closes above 0.786) have ZERO edge. This means the strategy is inherently selective — it only trades the most extreme retracements.

5. Wick/body ratio = 1.8x avg: These are candles with large wicks relative to bodies, which is where the fib divergence between body and wick measures comes from.

6. Sample period: 239 days (202502–202602). Results need out-of-sample validation.

7. Trade-through assumption: We require price to trade 1 tick through the 0.786 level for a fill. Real market orders may face additional slippage beyond this.
