What is the "FVG"? Inverse FVG — gap forms with the sweep direction (bullish FVG after sweep high), then price closes back through it. The "inversion" IS the entry signal. Regular FVG — gap forms in the reversal direction after the sweep. Price is expected to pull back into the FVG edge for entry. The FVG is a zone, not a confirmation event.

Portfolio Verdict

The two strategies are uncorrelated (r = 0.046) because they detect fundamentally different market events. IFVG catches fast micro-sweeps on 1-min bars and enters on momentum (candle closes through FVG). Lumi waits for larger hourly sweeps, requires a three-step confirmation chain (MSS → OB → FVG), and enters passively at a limit level. Even when they trade on the same day near the same price, they agree on direction only ~50% of the time — essentially coin-flip overlap. This makes Lumi a strong diversification add to the Tempo portfolio.

The Two Key Philosophical Differences
#IFVGLUMI
1 Confirmation-first: "I'll enter AFTER price proves the reversal by closing through the FVG. I pay worse fills but I know the move is happening." Anticipation-first: "I'll place a limit at the FVG edge and wait for price to come to me. I get better fills but the move might never materialize."
2 Survive with soft stops: "Wicks will try to stop me out but I only exit on a candle CLOSE past my stop. The edge IS in the stop type — 50% of 'losers' never actually close past my stop." Survive with structure: "I need MSS + OB + FVG all confirmed before I even place my order. The edge IS in the filter chain — most sweeps never produce all three confirmations."
