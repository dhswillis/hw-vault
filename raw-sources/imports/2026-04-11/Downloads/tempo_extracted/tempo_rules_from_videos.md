## 1. ENTRY RULES

### IFVG (Inverted Fair Value Gap) Setup
- **Primary entry mechanism** — 3-candle pattern where middle candle creates a gap
- **Optimal gap size**: 8–15 points on NQ (5-point gaps considered risky)
- **Avoid** multiple gaps stacked (increases failure probability)
- Reject FVG into IFVG = strong confidence setup
- Singular gaps preferred over multiple confluences
- Enter on **close** above/below gap (depending on direction)

### Confirmation Patterns
- V-shaped recovery with instant momentum
- Death candles through gaps (80–100 point candles = high volatility)
- Wicks rejecting at levels without closing past = major confidence signal
- Three red candles in a row after sweep = strong bearish reaction
- Instant green candles after low sweep = bullish reversal signal

### A/B Setup Classification
- **A+ Setup**: Major sweep + SMT divergence + IFVG + daily bias alignment
- **B Setup**: Missing one confluence factor — trade with reduced size
- **C Setup**: Missing multiple factors — SKIP entirely

### Data Wick Setup (News-Based)
- Triggered by: Triple red folder news (8:30 CPI, NFP, etc.)
- Formation: One-minute candle with wick on BOTH ends (top and bottom)
- Ideal data wick: 50–100 points top-to-bottom
- Entry: Wait for ONE side to fully sweep
- Exit: Target opposite end of wick

## 2. EXIT / TRIM RULES

### Trim Strategy (Multiple Exits on NQ)
- **TP1**: 15–20 points profit → trim 20–50% of position
- **TP2**: 30–40 points profit → trim additional 20–40%
- **TP3**: 50–70 points profit → trim 20–30%
- **Final runners**: Hold remaining micros for 70–120+ point moves
- "One micro runner" = holding smallest contract size for potential extended move

### Named Targets
- Swing highs/lows (relative equal highs/lows on timeframe)
- Data high/low (from data wick — 50–100 point targets)
- 50% of daily range (marked as yellow line, major bounce area)
- 50% of four-hour FVG
- London high/low (prior session overnight high/low)
- Asia high/low
- New York AM high of day
- Previous day high/low
- Trend line liquidity above/below current price

### Profit Target Notes
- When price goes 1–4 points past target then reverses = manipulation
- Second trim rarely hits — focus on first trim lock-in
- Philosophy: "1 win per day is enough"

## 3. STOP LOSS RULES

### Placement
- Stop loss at **close above/below gap** OR at swing high/low
- **FVG close-to-close stop**: Usually 20–35 points
- **Swing high/low stop**: Usually 10–25 points
- **Tight stops**: 5–8 points (risky, get wicked easily — avoid)
- **Order block stop**: Usually 10–20 points
- Can extend stop to include additional PD array below/above main gap

### Break-Even Rules (Critical)
- Go break even once **15–20 points** profit secured (non-negotiable)
- Alternative trigger: Hit internal swing high/low after entry
- "Manipulation prevention": Tight stops get wicked out, BE at 20pts saves account
- Result: What looked like loss becomes zero, preventing major drawdowns
- Examples: Saved from 30–40 point losses by going break even at 20 points

## 4. DAILY BIAS DETERMINATION

### Premium/Discount Framework
- Draw line from daily high to daily low, mark **50% midpoint**
- Price **ABOVE 50%** = PREMIUM (short bias for reversion)
- Price **BELOW 50%** = DISCOUNT (long bias for reversion)
- Price **AT 50%** = NO BIAS (wait for sweep)
- Bounces at 50% level common, reliable support/resistance

### Bias Rules
- First sweep direction determines ~70% of daily bias
- Open in premium + first sweep of high = bearish day likely
- Open in discount + first sweep of low = bullish day likely
- Shorting back down to 50% from premium = ideal setup
- Longing back up to 50% from discount = ideal setup
- Do NOT long heavily in deep premium without major sweep below
- Do NOT short heavily in deep discount without major sweep above

## 5. SESSION / TIME RULES

### New York Session (Primary Trading Window)
- Session open: **9:30 AM EST**
- Ideal trading window: **9:30 AM – 11:00 AM** (AM Window)
- Afternoon session: 12:30 PM onwards (lunchtime window — less volatile)
- "Killzone": 2:00 PM window (news/volatility zone, risky)
- Avoid: 30 minutes before/after major news

### Pre-Market & Asia
- Asia session: 6:00 PM – 6:00 AM (rare trades, high risk)
- Pre-market: 6:00 AM – 9:30 AM (trades possible, prefer post-open confirmation)
- Avoid: Data wicks in pre-market (don't trade until market opens officially)

### News Windows
- **8:30 AM**: Red Folder News (CPI, NFP, triple red folder — creates DATA WICKS)
- **10:00 AM**: Consumer Sentiment
- **2:00 PM**: FOMC minutes, Powell speaking (high volatility, avoid/size down)

### Calendar Rules
- **Monday (no news)**: Often choppy, tight ranges (70–100 point AM ranges = bad)
- **Bank Holiday**: Low liquidity, tight ranges, DO NOT TRADE
- **Election weeks**: Manipulation, chop (watch but don't overexpose)
- **Last 2 weeks December**: Historically worst conditions all year
- **First week January**: Conditions improve, more volume
- **Black Friday/Holiday**: Thin volume, avoid if possible

## 6. SMT (Smart Money Technique) RULES

### Core Concept
- SMT = NQ vs ES divergence at key levels
- When one index sweeps liquidity but the other doesn't = divergence signal

### Specific Signals
- **Bullish SMT**: NQ sweeps low, ES doesn't = longs valid
- **Bearish SMT**: ES sweeps high, NQ doesn't = shorts valid
- **Open SMT**: Strongest signal — divergence visible from session open
- Confluence: SMT + major sweep + IFVG = A-plus setup

## 7. RISK MANAGEMENT

### Position Sizing
- **50K funded account**: 10–15 micro contracts typical
- **150K funded account**: 15–20 micro contracts
- Risk per trade: **$150–370** typical loss threshold (0.4–0.6% of account)
- NOT 1% — FAQ confirmed 0.4–0.6% ($200–300 on $50k)

### Loss Management
- **Stop after 2 consecutive losses** for the day
- Max loss per trade: 10–35 points typical
- After loss: Wait for A-setup before next trade (not just any setup)
- No revenge trading — no scaling up after drawdown

### Size Adjustments
- **Half size** in bad conditions / choppy markets / thin volume
- Different strategy for evals (sometimes riskier since not personal capital)

## 8. NEGATIVE CONFLUENCES (When to SKIP)

- Gap > 30 points (too much risk for stop loss)
- Multiple gaps stacked (above and below entry)
- Too close to news event (within 2 minutes)
- Already 2+ losses in day
- Setup doesn't have SMT or major sweep confirmation
- Chop/range trading conditions (70–100 point range in 2+ hours)
- Trader already taken profit target for day ("already green")
- Swept all overnight areas before market open = worst conditions
- No clear bias, 50% level not respected

## 10. TRADE MANAGEMENT

### During Trade
- Trim 30–50% at first level (15–20 points)
- Trail stop in 10–15 point increments after first trim
- Break even after 20 points profit = non-negotiable
- Hold runners with wider stops (swing-based)

### Special Patterns
- **Power of Three**: Accumulation → Manipulation → Distribution
- **Order Block Reentry**: After initial IFVG failure, down candle creates OB → reentry works
- **BPR (Overlapping FVGs)**: When hourly gaps overlap → major reversal potential

### Chase Rules (High Risk)
- Clean gap, first rejection only, strong momentum continues
- Wait 5+ minutes from open (let market settle)
- Half size only
- Immediate exit if reverses back into gap
- Rare scenario: Only when high probability confluence present

## 11. CORE PRINCIPLES

1. **Liquidity Sweep = Truth**: Every major move follows liquidity sweep, never ignore
2. **Reactions Matter Most**: How price reacts to sweep tells you if reversal coming
3. **Momentum Over Candle Pattern**: Aggressive movement through gap > candle shape
4. **SMT Confirmation**: Divergence between ES and NQ adds major confluence
5. **One Trade = One Green Day**: "1 win per day is enough"
6. **Break Even Saves Accounts**: Going BE at 20 points more valuable than chasing 100
7. **Never Chase Pre-Market**: Too unpredictable until official market open
8. **Premium/Discount Rules**: Price doesn't stay in premium or discount indefinitely
9. **Wait > Force**: Waiting for A-setup > forcing B/C setups
10. **Trim Early, Hold Runners**: Lock profits early (15–20 pts), let few contracts run
